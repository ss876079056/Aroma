Unbound

    hese pieces are experiments with fast, free brush strokes, different materials, and unique paint application methods. 
    In the painting on the left, I relaxed my wrist to use a greater range of motion with my brushstrokes. I used yellow, white and black as the primary colours to create a sense of conflict enhanced by the harsh lines created by the brush. 
    In the middle painting, I created two focus areas with contrasting colors in the upper left and lower right corners. Then, I connected the two points with straight, fast strokes, imagining that I was creating a passage through time and space between two parallel spaces.
    In the painting on the right, instead of using traditional brushes, I used twisted pieces of paper and soft cloth to brush on paint, squares of corrugated paper to stamp on paint, and pasted on small pieces of gold foil.
    The three pictures show my freedom and happiness in experimenting with how different wrist movement and materials can create more possibilities.
